STATUS: DRAFT_FOR_USER_APPROVAL

> NPC dialogue is an I3_FULL_FREEDOM surface. This pack deliberately pushes generation identity hard while preserving role intent and gameplay behavior. It is creative review material, not Codex implementation authority.

## 1. Design rule

The same NPC role must remain mechanically identical across profiles.

Only presentation changes:

- vocabulary;
- rhythm;
- cultural register;
- slang;
- humour;
- emoji/code density;
- level of explicitness.

A bandit remains a bandit. A cop remains a cop. A toxic NPC remains toxic. The tone profile does not change threat level, AI decisions, rewards, penalties, cooldowns, targets or outcomes.

## 2. Why the old NPC corpus fails Step 9

Historical seed examples show mostly paired voices:

- Millennial/Boomer bandit: `кошелек ближе`, `плати и уходи`
- Zoomer/Alpha bandit: `кошелек сюда`, `плати и вышел`

Similar pairing appears across cop, crowd, mafia and toxic roles.

Step 9 requires five independently recognisable cultural renderings.

# NPC DIVERSITY RULE

Do not make every NPC of one generation sound identical.

Tone profile defines the generation lane, while role/personality still creates variation.

Within each profile, later implementation should preserve at least these dimensions where the current architecture supports them:

- role;
- gender/identity-independent wording where possible;
- aggression level;
- confidence;
- verbosity;
- humour/dryness;
- social status/role vocabulary.

Forbidden failure mode:

> Every Zoomer says `бро`, every Alpha says `aura`, every Gen X says `чувак`, every Millennial mentions ICQ, every Boomer says `молодой человек`.

Those are signature markers, not mandatory prefixes.

# CROSS-GENERATION HIDDEN-LABEL TEST

For each NPC role, select 5 random lines from each generation without labels.

PASS only if:

- Boomer and Gen X are separable;
- Gen X and Millennial are separable;
- Millennial and Zoomer are separable;
- Zoomer and Alpha are separable;
- the role itself remains recognisable across all five profiles;
- no generation requires its label to explain the joke/slang;
- no line changes mechanic meaning or threat/action semantics.
