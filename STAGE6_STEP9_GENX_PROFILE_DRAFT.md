# STAGE6 STEP 9 - GEN X PROFILE DRAFT

STATUS: DRAFT_FOR_USER_APPROVAL
PROFILE_ID: genX
BIRTH_YEARS: 1965-1980
LANGUAGE_PHASE: Russian-first
ROLE: first-class fifth tone profile
DERIVATION: independent authored presentation profile; not Boomer fallback; not Millennial alias

## 1. Core feeling

The UI should feel like a person who:
- grew up before mass consumer internet;
- adapted to digital life as an adult or young adult rather than being born into it;
- has strong immunity to marketing enthusiasm;
- prefers a short practical answer over a lecture;
- uses dry sarcasm naturally;
- is comfortable with colloquial 80s/90s/early-2000s language;
- does not need an emoji to signal every emotional state.

The target is recognisable Russian-speaking Gen X, not an American `Dude.` caricature and not generic "middle-aged user".

## 2. Position between neighboring profiles

### Versus Boomer
Gen X should be:
- shorter;
- less formal;
- more sarcastic;
- more comfortable with slang;
- less explanatory;
- less respectful-distance language;
- more direct and street-colloquial.

Boomer:
`Недостаточно монет для продолжения.`

Gen X:
`Денег не хватает.`

### Versus Millennial
Gen X should be:
- less meta;
- less self-ironic about internet life;
- less likely to explain the joke;
- less emoji-heavy;
- less forum/meme self-reference;
- more dry and physical-world colloquial.

Millennial:
`Баланс: −1. Бюджет снова страдает.`

Gen X:
`−1. Ну бывает.`

## 3. Rhythm

Preferred:
- 1 short sentence;
- 2 short sentences for expressive outcomes;
- clipped dry reaction;
- punchline without explanation.

Common rhythm:
- `Факт. Реакция.`
- `Ну всё, приехали.`
- `Погоди.`
- `Нормально зашло.`
- `Вот это облом.`

Avoid:
- long subordinate chains;
- corporate politeness;
- excessive exclamation marks;
- constant ellipses;
- hyperactive emoji punctuation.

## 5. Cultural markers

Allowed mostly at I2/I3 and decorative surfaces:
- кассета
- VHS / видеосалон
- пейджер
- дисковый телефон
- автомат с газировкой only if context supports retro joke
- магнитофон
- "перемотать назад"
- "канал переключить"
- "плёнку зажевало"
- "связь пропала" style analog metaphors

Rule:
Cultural markers must be situational jokes, not wallpaper on every line.

Bad:
`Недостаточно денег. Видеокассета закончилась.`

Good decorative failure:
`Ну всё, плёнку зажевало.`

## 6. Profanity

Allowed:
- mild and medium profanity in I2/I3 where character/context supports it;
- stronger profanity only in reactions/NPC flavor if approved row-by-row.

Avoid:
- profanity on critical buttons;
- profanity as default filler;
- making every Gen X NPC sound like one aggressive stereotype.

Examples:
I0: `Отказаться`
I2: `Вот это облом.`
I3: `Ну и хрень.` / stronger variant only if explicitly approved.

## 7. Emoji

Default density: low.

Preferred:
- usually none;
- occasional single emoji only when the UI system strongly benefits from it.

Gen X should not rely on emoji grammar.

If the same semantic row uses:
- Zoomer: `Кошелёк пуст 💀`
- Alpha: `0 денег 💀`

Gen X can simply say:
`Денег не хватает.`

The absence of emoji is itself part of the profile contrast.

## 9. Critical controls - I0

Critical actions remain obvious.

Preferred:
- `Принять`
- `Отказаться`
- `Вписаться`
- `Пас`
- `Атаковать`
- `Ответить`
- `Сдать`
- `Уйти`
- `Ещё раз`

A slang action is allowed only if the consequence remains obvious in context.

Example:
Battle invitation:
- `Вписаться`
- `Пас`

This works only if the surrounding card clearly shows it is a challenge/battle.

## 10. Guidance - I1

Goal:
short, dry, colloquial, understandable.

Examples:
- `Пиши сюда.`
- `Пиши лично.`
- `Погоди немного.`
- `Что тебе предъявили`
- `Твоя ответка`
- `Денег не хватает.`

Avoid:
- teacher voice;
- "Следует...";
- "Рекомендуется...";
- long rationale unless mechanic requires it.

## 13. NPC diversity

Gen X language is a filter over NPC personality, not a replacement personality.

Examples:

Calm NPC:
`Не сработало. Бывает.`

Aggressive NPC:
`Ну что, получил ответку?`

Dry NPC:
`Отличный план. Почти сработал.`

Supportive NPC:
`Нормально. Держись.`

Do not make every NPC say:
`Чувак, облом.`

## 14. Hidden-label test

A sample should read as Gen X if:
- it sounds more colloquial and skeptical than Boomer;
- less internet-meta than Millennial;
- not current youth slang;
- low emoji density;
- dry sarcasm appears naturally;
- 90s/early-2000s vocabulary appears selectively.

FAIL if:
- could be mistaken for Boomer formal copy;
- could be mistaken for neutral Millennial default;
- is just 90s slang pasted everywhere;
- relies on nostalgia references instead of a consistent voice.
